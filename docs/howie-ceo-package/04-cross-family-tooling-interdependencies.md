## The Central Question

Document 03 established that like-kind manufacturing can seed Classic and Sculpt without starting from scratch. This document asks the harder follow-on question: **if ZELEX commissions 2–3 new skeleton frames, which frame geometries generate the most cross-family yield?**

The answer lies in bridge frames — body geometries that sit dimensionally close to two or more family centroids simultaneously. A bridge frame investment doesn't activate one family; it activates several. Understanding which bridges exist, where they overlap with ZELEX's current gaps, and how to sequence them converts tooling spend from a linear cost into a compounding capability.

### Reading the map

The six families cluster into two dimensional zones:

**Zone A — Defined waist, moderate curves (Classic / Sculpt / Muse):**  
WHR 0.63–0.67, BWR 1.32–1.46, Height 158–161cm. These three families share enough geometry that frames built for any one of them are adaptable to the others.

**Zone B — Strong curves, compressed waist (Icon / Empress / Siren):**  
WHR 0.58–0.60, BWR 1.53–1.76, Height 155–160cm. Empress and Siren are the extreme — Icon is the entry point to this zone.

ZELEX's current coverage: **Primary in Zone A via Muse.** Weak to absent in Classic (Zone A) and Sculpt (Zone A/transition). Moderate in Icon (Zone B), thin in Empress, absent in Siren. The structural play is to build Zone A depth first, then cascade into Zone B via the Icon/Empress bridge.

## Section 8 — What This Is Not

Two important scope limits:

**Siren is excluded from this sequence.** Siren's centroid (H155.5, W49.7, BWR 1.756) sits in Zone B extremes — the geometry required (extreme waist compression, very high bust-to-waist ratio) has no near-neighbor in ZELEX's current lineup. Siren requires a purpose-built frame commission, not a bridge frame. It's outside the like-kind manufacturing window.

**Muse is not a gap.** ZELEX's Muse family already has 18 bodies with 4–24% market share across all height bands. Investment in Muse framing would be incremental, not gap-filling. The capital case doesn't compete with Classic/Sculpt zero-to-one.

## Strategic Summary

The like-kind manufacturing opportunity is larger than two dead-end families. The data shows:

1. **ZG S155C and ZX163E are underpublished multi-family assets** — they already qualify for Classic, Sculpt, and Empress routing without new tooling. The cost is catalog work, not factory work.

2. **A single convergence frame at H160, W57** activates both Classic and Sculpt dead-ends with one commission — the market precedent (Gynoid GT 160) proves the geometry exists and is commercially validated.

3. **A second frame in the Sculpt/Icon bridge zone** reinforces ZELEX's highest-ROI active family (Icon) in its weakest height bands while simultaneously building Sculpt catalog depth — cross-family ROI from one tooling investment.

4. **The cascade is non-linear.** Two new frame commissions don't just fix two dead-end families — they reinforce Icon, enable Empress re-routing, and create Sculpt depth across three height bands. The interdependency multiplies the return on each tooling dollar.
